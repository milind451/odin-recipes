Shahi-Paneer recipe

It is the royal of India that we all love to eat and mostly prepared suring special occasions such as marriage or party.

  • 2 tablespoons chopped cashews
  • 1 cup finely chopped Onion
  • 1/2 cup Finely chopped Tomato
  • 1/4 cup Finely chopped Pepper
  • 1 tablespoon almonds
  • 1 tsp Red chili powder
  • 1 tsp Garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon melon seeds (magaz)
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • Paneer cubes
  • 2 cups Water

Instructions

  • Take the nuts and seeds 2 tablespoons chopped cashews, 1 tablespoon almonds, 1 tablespoon melon seeds (magaz) in a bowl and rinse them a couple of times with water.
  • Simmer the below listed ingredients in 1.5 cup water for for 8 to 10 minutes on medium-low heat.
  • Continue to cook until the onions become soft, then strain the stock and set aside for later use.
  • When cooled, place the softened onions, nuts, seeds, ginger, garlic in a blender or grinder-mixer jar. You can peel the almonds if you prefer.
  • Cook for about 4-5 minutes until tomatoes get cooked.
  • AStir and add the paneer cubes (200 grams) and 1 tablespoon heavy cream. Add 1 teaspoon of kewra water and stir (optional step). If adding butter, you can add it here. Mix well and switch off the heat.
  • Add water and salt. Mix well.
  • Taste makes contains salt. So add salt carefully.
  • Let the water come to boil.
  • Add dry coriander and mix well.
  • Cook for about 30 minutes until the water gets absorbed.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves or mint leaves or some saffron strands and enjoy with rice or flatbread.
